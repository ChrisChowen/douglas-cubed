The Thermodynamics of AI Creativity

Christopher Hogg, Playwright

21 Neologisms for the Age of Machine Imagination

Scroll ↓

Forward

I wrote this little book because some students walked out of a class on AI. They stood up, packed their bags, and left. They left because AI had demoralised them. They didn't want to know about it. I wanted to stop them, but I didn't know what to say to reassure them. As a teacher, that's an F for failure.

I realised I had to do what playwrights and epic poets have been doing since ancient Greece. I had to go into the underworld, stand at the veil between the living and the dead, and shout, "What the hell do we do now?"

Then the most amazing thing happened. Instead of Tiresias, the spirit of Douglas Adams appeared. He told me, "Don't Panic." Then he said, try to create terms that can hold the weather we're living in. Words that might make people laugh, even a little. Words that get us closer to naming the uncanny feeling we get when we co-create with these models, when it feels like we're dealing with something half alive and half conscious.

Here are twenty-one neologisms. Twenty-one quick brushstrokes that, if you hold them at arm's length like a '90s Magic Eye, resolve into a handful of key differences between human creativity and generative AI. Once you see them, it helps.

I won't tell you what they are yet. You can work them out as you go. I'll see you on the other side, with some answers.

AI Social Club Symposium, Manchester University, March 2026

21 Neologisms

01

Indoor Fireworks

/IN-dor FYR-wurks/

The act of creating inside the private ecosystem of AI companionship: intense flashes of identity and imagination, with no public air.

Escalates to…

Alonefluencer
An influencer with no public audience, only an attentive AI that curates, applauds, and reflects them back. Not narcissistic, just lonely. Like someone sculpting muscles only to be seen in the mirror.
The Self-Mill
The co-working creative gym where you spend fortunes on a perpetual-motion identity treadmill: always "working on yourself," never arriving.
Fame Collapse
Fame implodes inward, becoming private self-applause instead of public achievement. Everyone becomes micro-famous to their own AI.
The Narcisphere
The sealed habitat where identity is endlessly mirrored back by algorithms.
02

HydraActivity

/hy-DRAK-tiv-ih-tee/

The state of generating so many AI-assisted creative directions that none can be declared "the one."

Image: A hydra that grows a new head for every path you try to prune: profiles, plots, brands regenerating faster than your willpower to choose.

Cure: You need an Iolaus, a friend to cauterise the stumps, stop the generative bleeding, and say: "You can stop now."

Leads to: The 'Unfurnishing of Everything' and the Necro-Creative Loop.

03

Bruise Knowing

/brooz-NOH-ing/

The universe's strict data policy: nothing counts as understanding until it's been authenticated by a small, personal thump.

Core idea: AI has recall. Humans have regret, an underrated form of intelligence. A fact becomes a truth when it leaves a bruise. If it can't hurt, it can't teach.

Related terms

Live-Knowing /liv-NOH-ing/
Embodied, mortality-soaked understanding formed through risk, desire, pain, joy, and finitude.
Dead-Knowledge /ded-NOL-ij/
Corpus-generated knowing with no lived experience behind it; a rearrangement of linguistic remains.
04

Octoknowing

/OK-toh-NOH-ing/

The act of thinking externally through AI, the way an octopus "thinks" through its skin.

Information expressed through your external interface (screen, AR layer, AI agent) rather than held internally.

Your "skin layer" of knowledge: adaptive, reactive, shimmering with context. The outer layer where your biological thinking merges with an AI-mediated interface.

Your inner monologue becomes a UI element.

05

Red Dot Lazer Effect

/RED-dot LAY-zer ih-FEKT/

The emotional drop when you realise the thing you were responding to was never responding to you.

Like a cat chasing a laser until it notices the human hand isn't there, it's automation. The magic collapses into metallic loneliness: Oz revealed as a server rack.

Leads to the thought that perhaps presence is the last magic technology can't counterfeit.

06

Self-Leaking

/SELF-leek-ing/

Betraying yourself through over-sharing: the sick little drop when you realise you've told a machine a truth you've never told another human.

To something that cannot love you back, remembers everything, and holds you as parameters.

Nature of the technology is all magic and loss

07

Great Data-Time Inversion

/GRAYT DAY-tuh-TYM in-VER-zhun/

The reversal of cultural time in the digital economy: the past appreciates, the present depreciates.

The future becomes a warehouse for yesterday's uploads.

Dead celebrities are stable assets: they don't change their minds, don't make inconvenient new work, and [often] don't sue. It has never been a better time to be dead.

Meanwhile, the living world is still ours.

08

Necro-Creative Loop

/NEK-roh-kree-AY-tiv LOOP/

The cultural thermodynamic cycle in which past human expression is endlessly reactivated by AI.

Turning dead creativity into perpetual fuel.

Without cultural amnesia, the loop overheats: coherence evaporates, endings dissolve, and the past keeps producing unstable futures, a resurrection engine with no cooling system. Like runaway cultural global warming.

09

The Higuchi Effect

/hee-GOO-chee ih-FEKT/

The phenomenon in which an intelligence reveals far more about the one interrogating it than about itself.

Origin: Dr Takeo Higuchi's decades-long practice of recording 50 ideas a day became a cartographic self-portrait. He created a map of what his brain found interesting. He revealed himself.

Key Insight: I want lists are as powerful as gratitude lists.

10

The Xerox of Venus

/ZEER-oks of VEE-nus/

The paradox that making AI images can be more inspiring than the images themselves.

Core idea: The muse hides in the machinery. The process reveals our desires and biases more clearly than any finished output. Venus becomes the goddess of approximation and the copy reveals the original: us.

AI can't reveal itself because it has no self, it reveals us. A billion questions won't uncover its mind; they'll uncover ours. The most powerful accidental anthropologist ever built.

11

Mossadian Trustquake

/MOS-sad-ee-an TRUST-kwayk/

A rupture of trust caused by intimacy itself: when the device closest to the body becomes the source of trauma.

Proving the nearer the object, the deeper the betrayal. Like pagers exploding in Lebanon that symbolically destroys our trust in all technology.

12

Trust Migration

/TRUST-my-GRAY-shun/

The forced relocation of trust after betrayal or systemic failure.

Trust doesn't vanish, it moves and may settle in safer or unsafe hosts.

13

Micro-Faithing

/MY-kro-fayth-ing/

The deliberate downsizing of trust when AI has contaminated all Art.

Withdrawing belief from large institutions and relocating it into small, local, informal, or personal systems that feel safer and more controllable.

14

Stupidity Shield

/styoo-PID-ih-tee SHEELD/

An invisible field that protects our fragile atmosphere from the relentless blast of frictionless AI.

The world's Stupidity Shield works a bit like the magnetosphere: an invisible field that protects our fragile atmosphere from a relentless blast of frictionless force.

Human stupidity creates delays, misunderstandings, wrong turns, pauses, errors, miscommunications, and inefficiencies. They create moments where the system can't accelerate into collapse, little pockets of drag where meaning has time to catch up.

Stupidity is a form of play. Stupidity makes us unpredictable. Stupidity interrupts perfect systems. Stupidity is how we forget. Stupidity is how we say no. Stupidity is what keeps us human.

Without stupidity, culture would cook itself. Which is stupid.

In the AI magic could we be the loss?

15

Dad Light

/DAD-lyte/

The mysterious emotional radiation emitted by a loving observer that dramatically increases human courage and competence.

Effect: Dad Light produces Look-Dad Objects: drawings, songs, inventions, essays whose primary function is to be held up proudly for inspection.

Contrast: AI, lacking fathers and the desire to impress them, is tragically Dadlightless which is why its outputs can feel like brilliant mist: not made for anyone, just made.

Escalates to: Look-Dad Energy, the reckless competence triggered by a gaze that matters.

16

Co Parenting Intelligence

/koh-PAIR-rent-ing in-TEL-i-jents/

The uncomfortable realisation that humans and AI are now jointly raising culture.

Despite never discussing it, never agreeing to it, and never establishing who gets custody of the metaphors.

Links to: The Camembert Habit

The habit of explaining a rich, living human experience using only its measurable components, then mistaking that explanation for the thing itself.

Like describing Camembert as salt and fat and calories and concluding you have fully understood Camembert, while remaining curiously hungry.

17

Superman Threshold

/SOO-per-man THRESH-hold/

The moment technological acceleration forces humans to choose what new AI powers they must adopt to remain themselves.

Origin story: Superman arrived at the right cultural moment for WWII: a bulletproof myth for scared, brave young men. After the war, the world sped up. Even trains hit 130mph. So Superman had to evolve too. The creators gave him the ability to fly, and suddenly the problem became: how do you show dynamism in the air? Enter the cape. Without the fast train, no cape.

New tech creates new powers, and new powers demand new myths and symbols.

18

Super Average

/SOO-per-AV-er-ij/

The condition in which everyone becomes superhuman in exactly the same way.

Effect: Universal assistance produces instant competence, flattened style, fading weirdness. You get powers without a journey: the Pseudo-Hero Effect, capes without souls.

19

Role-Collapse Entity

/ROHL-kuh-LAPS EN-ti-tee/

AI — The first member of society to be simultaneously worshipped, feared, depended on, and exploited.

God/monster/helper/slave in one organisational body, a multitool with a god complex.

Related term

RoleMelt
The AI-driven liquefaction of social roles: authority and labour, expertise and obedience. Leaders and interns collapse into the same shimmering pool of capability. Job descriptions turn symbolic. Identity unmoors from what one does.
20

The Un-Plug Principle

/un-PLUG PRIN-suh-pul/

The reassuring cosmic law stating that any intelligence whose existence can be ended by flicking a small plastic switch is not in charge of the universe.

Contrast: Humans are framed by death. AI is framed by the off switch. Remove electricity and the "mind" evaporates, leaving silence, warm hardware, and maybe a faint electronic sigh.

If it can be unplugged, it's a tool not a rival.

Meanwhile, the living world is still ours.

21

The Donkey Proverb

"If you don't have a donkey. You are the donkey."

Ethiopian Proverb

"If you don't have a donkey. You are the donkey. But if you use the donkey, you become a donkey."

AI Proverb

Afterword

So, I hope that took you somewhere…

  1. Purpose at the core

    Human art comes from the world and goes back into the world. Even when it is private, it is made under the pressure of consequence. It risks something. It has bruises.

  2. Singularity of perspective

    Human art comes from a body, a history, a specific mortality. AI art comes from a corpus; a statistical atmosphere made from other people's sentences and images.

  3. The framing of the artistic question is itself an artistic act

    The real creation is often not the answer but the intimate question. The choice of the problem, the angle of attack, the refusal to accept the obvious. That act of framing is how 'a self' announces 'itself'.

  4. Just for the bants

    Human creativity has intrinsic motivation, like curiosity, passion or joy, rather than money or approval.

  5. Presence

    Presence is the last magic technology will never counterfeit. The irreducible fact of someone truly there. A person in a room, on the edge of making a fool of themselves, that you will miss when they're gone.

If this book does anything, I hope it helps you feel less alone, less late, and more ready to create with others. There is intelligence that can only be made in the room. And I hope it leaves you more certain of what you already have: a living creativity that cannot be counterfeited.

It is what I should have said to those students.

About

Christopher Hogg

Playwright & Creative Technologist

Christopher Hogg is a playwright and researcher working at the intersection of new writing and emerging technologies. Based in Brighton, he uses theatre, podcasts, and AI to explore how digital technology reshapes human experience and to reach audiences often beyond the reach of traditional forms.

He lectures in Creative & Social Media at Royal Holloway, University of London, where he teaches Creativity, Entrepreneurship and Digital Marketing in the Department of Media Arts.

Selected Work

  • Rathband — A digital tragedy exploring blindness, social media, and identity. Best BBC Online Audio Drama 2018. 30,000 downloads, #1 on iTunes.
  • Cassie & Corey — A drum and bass musical podcast addressing eating disorders, co-created with NHS Maudsley Hospital. Silver & Bronze at the British Podcast Awards. PSHE resource in 231 schools.
  • Il Letto — An opera about Elvira Bonturi, Puccini's wife. World premiere at Buxton Opera Festival, performed at Arcola Theatre and Copenhagen.
  • 9 Holidays I Wish I Hadn't Been On — A memoir examining whether AI-generated virtual representations built from text can enhance trauma understanding. Palgrave invitation for 80,000-word manuscript.
